PRIVACY POLICY

SNO respects its members and website visitors as well its many affiliates. SNO is committed to treating private information about affiliates with respect by adhering to privacy and confidentiality policies explained herein. Below the following are addressed: SNO’s current process of defining and integrating privacy policy into SNO operations; disclosure of what information SNO collects, how SNO uses that information, how confidentiality is maintained with security; and how SNO’s policies align with current state, national and international policies, standards and rules.

Information SNO acquires and uses

SNO collects personal information to register new members or conference attendees and speakers. SNO uses this information to serve its members, potential members and the broader nano- science and technology community. SNO also acquires information voluntarily provided by all affiliates.

SNO’s legal counsel is engaged with Europe’s General Data Privacy Rule and continually strives to reduce the information we collect to the minimum necessary in order for us to meet its mission and all of its affiliates’ needs in a safe, secure manner. Here are some of the types of information we collect with specification of how we use this information:

Information you provide voluntarily

  • Account information, which may include name, username and password, contact information, professional interests, demographic information, preferences, and other information that you may provide in the context of specific products, such as conferences or membership. We use such information:
      To interact with our members and other affiliates.
    • To administer and sustain services (for example, to determine a member’s grade or to remind a member to renew his/her membership.)
    • To invite members and other affiliates to participate in activities such as polls, surveys and volunteer opportunities; or to enable others to connect with you.
    • To enable social networking, for example by participating in a discussion or volunteer opportunity.
    • To include member information in an online profile and directories to enable people to find and connect with each other.
    • To inform members, via email and other channels, of SNO’s products and services that may be of interest to them, including conferences, education, publications and membership.
  • Payment data that members and affiliates provide when they purchase a product or service, such as your credit card number. We use this information to process payments. We employ the industry best practices such as Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS) to process payments.
  • Information voluntarily submitted by members and affiliates in polls and surveys (for example, the professional salary surveys) and other forms. In polls and surveys, member and affiliate responses are only reported in aggregate to fulfil the survey’s goals, without identifying individuals. In other forms, we use member and affiliate information in line with the form’s objective.
